Understanding Color Mixing

Additive Color Mixing (Light): When mixing colored light (like on screens), colors combine additively. Red, green, and blue are the primary additive colors. Combining all three at full intensity creates white light.

Subtractive Color Mixing (Paints): When mixing paints or pigments, colors combine subtractively. Cyan, magenta, and yellow are the primary subtractive colors. Combining all three absorbs all light, resulting in black or dark brown.

How this tool works: This visualizer averages the RGB values of your three colors to simulate what happens when you mix them. This gives you an approximation of both additive and subtractive mixing.
