Experiment Notes: Ohm's Law

Objective: To verify Ohm's Law which states that the current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points, and inversely proportional to the resistance between them.

Formula: V = I × R

  • V = Voltage (in Volts)
  • I = Current (in Amperes)
  • R = Resistance (in Ohms)

Procedure:

  1. Adjust the voltage using the voltage slider
  2. Adjust the resistance using the resistance slider
  3. Click "Calculate Current" to apply Ohm's Law
  4. Observe how current changes with voltage and resistance

Observation: When resistance is constant, current increases with voltage. When voltage is constant, current decreases with resistance.
